Wildlife Attractions

Wildlife attractions provide an opportunity to interact with wild animals or observe them in their natural habitats from far away.
These attractions can include interactions with captive animals such as elephant trekking, visits to animal sanctuaries which house rescued wild animals, street performances such as snake charming, or a safari to observe animals in their natural habitats.

Interactions with wild animals

A direct, hand-to-paw interaction with a wild animal can be terrifying and exciting at the same time.
In Sri Lanka the go to place for these kind of interactions with wild animals are zoos. The most notable and popular zoos in Sri Lanka for this are the Dehiwala Zoo and the Pinnawala Zoo.

Dehiwala Zoo

Dehiwala zoo, most commonly known as the Colombo zoo, established in 1936 is one of the oldest zoo in all of Asia. The zoo has a variety of animals and birds, and places an emphasis on animal conservation and welfare, and education.

Currently, Dehiwala Zoo houses 72 species of mammals, 65 species of birds, 31 species of reptiles, 89 species of fish, 03 families of amphibians and 30 species of butterflies. The total number of animals varies from 2500-3000 due to breeding and free living colonies of birds.
Dehiwala zoo is famous among visitors for its collection of local and exotic animals and its lush green landscapes which blend seamlessly with the habitats of the animals.

Activities at Dehiwala Zoo

The zoo hosts three animal shows which allows the visitors to interact with animals.

The most prominent one is the elephant show which has elephants perform at the elephant arena in the eveing. The elephants perform antics such standing on their heads, wiggling their backs to music, hopping on one foot and standing up on their hind legs.

The next performance is an educational programme on chimps. The zookeepers bring a hand-raised chimpanzee baby and allow visitors to touch and hold it while educating the visitors on chimp behaviours and about the chimp species as a whole.

Finally, a sea lion performs daily at the sea lions' pool in front of an audience.

Pinnawala Zoo

Pinnawala Zoo, also called the Pinnawala Open Zoo, opened very recently in 2015.
The zoo is home to many endemic animals in Sri Lanka and animals such as chital, lion, Sri Lankan Leopard, purple-faced leaf monkey, wild boar, sambar, asian elephant, and different endemic bird species.

This zoo is famous for a particular concept it has adopted known as open zoo, and is the first open-air zoo in Sri Lanka. According to this concept the animals are housed in open enclosures while the visitors observe them from behind glass cages.
The zoo has seven main enclosures with an internal road network connecting the enclosures. The seven main enclosures are pet area, swan pond, deer enclosure, crocodile enclosure, leopard enclosure, bear enclosure, and butterfly garden.

Animal Sanctuaries

Animal sanctuaries provide care for rescued animals and look after them for the rest of their lives. In a sanctuary the residents come first, as such a sanctuary eliminates any activity or entity that would stress out the animals.
This in turn provides an authentic view into the behaviour and daily life routines of the animals.

In Sri Lanka, the most popular wildlife sanctuary is the Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage.

Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage

The Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage is an orphanage, nursery and captive breeding ground for wild Asian elephants.
Established in 1975 by the Sri Lanka Department of Wildlife Conservation (DWC) the Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage was founded to care and protect the many orphaned, unweaned wild elephants found wandering in and near the forests of Sri Lanka.
Currently the orphanage cares for 93 elephants.

Activities at Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage

Everyday the elephants are taken to the nearby river, the "Ma Oya", and then back again to the orphanage to be fed. Since its a sanctuary the visitors are allowed to view the care and daily routine of the elephants from a distance without interrupting or interacting with the elephants.

Observing wildlife

This includes observing a occurrence or abundance of animal species at a specific location and time with minimal disturbance to the animals.
Common examples of this in Sri Lanka is leopard spotting, going on a wildlife safari, and whale watching.

Yala National Park

Yala National Park is the second largest national park in Sri Lanka and has one highest leopard densities in the world.
The park is home to 215 bird species of which 7 are endemic to Sri Lanka. They are Sri Lanka grey hornbill, Sri Lanka junglefowl, Sri Lanka wood pigeon, crimson-fronted barbet, black-capped bulbul, blue-tailed bee-eater and brown-capped babbler. Other animals in the park include elephants, Sri Lankan sloth bear, leopard, wild water buffalo, toque macaque, golden palm civet, red slender loris, and fishing cat. Many reptiles, amphibians, fish, crabs, prawns, and butterflies also reside in the park.

Since the park has a variety of ecosystems such as moist monsoon forests, dry monsoon forests, semi deciduous forests, thorn forests, grasslands, marshes, marine wetlands, and sandy beaches, Yala is not only home to many different kinds of animals but also to different kinds of floral species such as red mangrove, mangrove apple,black mangrove, Drypetes sepiaria, Ceylon satinwood, arjun tree, wood apple tree, Australian cowplant, bell mimosa, neem, banyan, toothbrush tree, and much more.

Activities at Yala National Park

With 215 bird species in the park, bird watching allows to study these flying creatures and be mesmorised by them. But a safari ride around the park is the most sought out by visitors since it is an experience of a lifetime.
A safari ride around the park can catch the inhabitants of the jungle in there natural habitats and it is feeling of uncertainty and adventure that makes it an exciting and adventurous wildlife experience.

Mirissa

Mirissa is a small town on the south coast of Sri Lanka.
It is popular for its golden, sandy beaches and nightlife, but what captivates the interest of most nature lovers is because Mirissa is the island's main whale and dolphin watching location.

In fact, Mirissa is ranked as the top ten whale-watching destinations in the world. Most people who go on whale watching in Mirissa are likely to catch the glimpse of a blue or a sperm whale since there are tons of krill around here that the whales feast on, and there's an annual migration of blue and sperm whales between the Bay of the Bengal and the Arabian Sea, via the coast of Sri Lanka.

Red mangrove Plantae Red mangroves are a small to medium size evergreen tree growing to a height of about 20 to 25 m. The tree has a large number of aerial stilt roots buttressing the trunk. The leaves are elliptical and usually about 12 cm long and 6 cm wide. There are corky warts on the pale undersides of the leaves. Red mangrove is found in the Indo-Pacific region on the banks of rivers and on the edge of the sea. Their natural habitat are estuaries, tidal creeks and flat coastal areas subject to daily tidal flooding. It is used to help prevent coastal erosion and in restoration of mangrove habitats. The timber is used for firewood and in the construction of buildings, as poles and pilings, and in making fish traps. The fruits can be cooked and eaten or the juice extracted to make wine, and the young shoots can be consumed as a vegetable. The bark is used in tanning and a dye can be extracted from both bark and leaves. Various parts of the plant are used in traditional medicine.
Mangrove apple Plantae Mangrove apple is a type of tree which can grow upto 20 m in height and with a trunk reaching a maximum diameter of 50 cm, and it bears green and red colored fruits. It is present in Africa, Indonesia, northeast Australia, New Caledonia, Hainan Island in China, and the Philippines. It is present in tropical tidal mud flats. The leaves and the fruit are edible and are used as food in certain countries. The roots of the plant are sometimes used to make fishing net floats.
Black mangrove Plantae Black mangrove grows as a shrub or small tree up to 7 m high. It has clusters of fragrant, small, white flowers. The fruit is curved and cylindrical or horn-shaped, light green to pink in colour. It grows in mud in estuaries and tidal creeks in many regions around the world. Black mangrove is used as medcine.
Arjun tree Plantae Arjun tree grows to about 20-25 metres tall. It has a buttressed trunk, and forms a wide canopy at the crown, from which branches drop downwards. It has oblong, conical leaves which are green on the top and brown below, and smooth, grey bark. It bears woody, fibrous fruits. The tree is seen across the Indian Subcontinent, and usually found growing on river banks or near dry river beds. The tree is used in silk production.
Australian cowplant Plantae The plant is a climber with leaves having soft hairs on the upper surface. The leaves are elongated-oval in shape. The plant is found in tropical Asia, China, the Arabian peninsula, Africa and Australia. Its habitat is that of secondary forest, river forest and dry shrub savannah, generally on sandy or clayey soils. The plant is used in Ayurvedic medicine.
